Things about keshi:

  • Real Name - Casey Luong
  • Ethnicity - Vietnames
  • November 4, 1994 - Born in Huston, Texas.
  • Reason for his stage name - It is because of his Fiance's parents who is a Japanese Descent and instead of calling him Casey they mispronounce his name to keshi.
  • Proffesion - He used to be a nurse but when his carrer in music became big he stopped being a nurse.
  • How he started music - He started music with a ukelele because his grandparent gave him one because he wanted a guitar, but the parents didn't want him to have one.
  • Sports - He used to play Tennis.
  • How his career started - It was in soundcloud he uploads his music there until people used his music in streams.
  • 2018 - He released some of his songs on spotify, youtube, itunes.
  • 2018 - first ep (Extended Play) released named the reaper
  • 2019 - start of the Trilogy Ep named skeletons
  • 2020 - 2nd part of the Trilogy Ep released named bandaids
  • October 2020 - last part of the Trilogy Ep released named always
  • September 1, 2021 - He was one of the people who collaborated in the Shang-Chi and the Legend of the Ten Rings Soundtrack with the son War with Heaven
  • November 6-7, 2021 - He was part in the event Head in the Clouds Los Angeles Music & Arts Festival
  • Febuary 2022 - He will release his album GABRIEL

"I feel like the whole Asian identity part to it gets stressed a lot, you know. Wanting Asian representation in media– I think that’s great. But for me, personally, I feel like the bigger part, the more important part to me is making good art. Whether I’m Asian or not shouldn’t have anything to do with it. And I know that’s kinda what you guys are going for which makes a lot of sense, but I think that when you try to stress that you are an Asian-American artist, it’s almost like you’re trying to carve out a space for yourself that says, “Hey, this guy is an Asian artist so you should give him attention because of that”, when the real reason you should be giving someone attention is because their art is just good. I’ve strayed a little bit away from doing those kinds of organizations because I didn’t want to be labeled as an Asian-American artist, I just want to be an artist. I think that’s where I found success, just doing my thing. "

-- Casey (keshi) Luong
